Dr. Mike Moses is Awarded the Mirabeau B. Lamar Award at Duck Creek Lodge

Brethren,

Last night we presented the Mirabeau B. Lamar Award to Dr. Mike Moses. We had a decent turn out between our Lodge Family and Dr. Moses' friends, which included the famous Homer B. Johnson.

Our Stewards, John Puckett and David Carter, produced a spread of absolutely wonderful food including pork chops (2 inches thick) and a selection of desserts to die for. I would like to personally praise the successful efforts of Brother Puckett and Brother Carter. We might have served everyone on paper plates and with plastic silverware, but what they put on those plates was excellent. Thank you very much John and David, I am very proud of the work y'all are doing for the Lodge.

Dr. Moses was born on October 12, 1951 to Dr. & Mrs. Morgan Moses of Longview, Texas (at that time). Mike's dad, Morgan, came to the Masonic Home and School in Fort Worth, Texas around 1928 and was there until 1940 or 1941. After separating from the Home and School, Morgan Moses joined the Army Air Corp and served his country proudly during World War II. Returning home after the war, Morgan married Marie Frances and began teaching in 1948. Somewhere in there Morgan was made a Master Mason. In the mid 50's Morgan moved his family to Garland to be the principal at Garland High School. Many of you will remember Morgan. He left GHS for Stephen F. Austin University in 1964 to become Dean of the Education Program. Nacogdoches and SFA were good to the Moses family. Dr. Mike Moses graduated high school and attended college classes there as well. Later on Mike would receive his Doctorate in Education Administration from East Texas State University. Dr. Mike Moses would receive more than just an education as a young man being raised by Dr. Morgan Moses. Morgan and Marie clearly instilled strong morals, never ending ethics, everlasting compassion, a sense of humor, and most of all, they gave Monte and Mike a lot of love. It is also apparent Morgan shared his passion for Education with Mike and Monte.

Dr. Mike Moses began teaching history for Duncanville ISD in 1974, but transferred to Garland ISD as an Assistant Principal in 1976 and was quickly made Principal in 1977 where he remained until 1982. During that time I was a student at Brandenburg Middle School in Garland, where Dr. Moses was Principal, and he just happen to live across the alley from me and my family. While attending Brandenburg I would notice Dr. Moses and witness his character, though it would be more than 20 years before I would fully understand what it was he had actually done for me. That in mind, I have no doubt he was the same to everyone. From there he became the Superintendent of Tatum ISD, La Marque ISD, and Lubbock ISD. Dr. Moses was appointed Commissioner of the Texas Education Agency. In 1999 he returned to Lubbock as Deputy Chancellor of Texas Tech University before coming home to Dallas in 2001 to be Superintendent of Dallas ISD where he remained until 2004. Dr. Moses is currently sitting on several boards of directors and consults for numerous school districts around the state and country.

While Dr. Moses' resume alone would most likely qualify him to received the Lamar Award, it is his character and passion that actually qualifies him (in my opinion). Dr. Mike Moses was made a Master Mason in October of 1981 at Garland Lodge #441. Dr. Moses has clearly put his Masonic teachings to use in his life and continues to use them to this day. It is one thing to put great effort into the education of others, but it is a much higher level compassion and caring to actual continue to worry decades later whether or not he had a positive effect on those students lives. Dr. Moses has always been more concerned with making sure his students got a solid, competitive, and complete education. His service to this Community and this State and as a result, the Country at large, is immeasurable.

Morgan taught Mike what it was to be a Man and showed Mike what it is to be a Mason. Brother Williams of Garland #441 taught Mike what is to be a Mason and Mike learned what it is to be an Educator. Dr. Mike Moses has showed us all what it is to be an Authentic Human, a Man, a Mason, and what Education should be about, the children. We could say for those reasons we gave Dr. Moses the Lamar Award, but I would say to you all that it is for those reasons Dr. Mike Moses EARNED the Mirabeau B. Lamar Award.

Duck Creek Lodge #1419 is proud to have presented Dr. Michael A. Moses with the Mirabeau B. Lamar Award.

Sincerely Yours,

Bob Keltner, Worshipful Master
Duck Creek Lodge #1419

Mike Moses currently holds the Distinguished Meadows Visiting Professorship in Educational Leadership at Texas A&M University-Commerce. Dr. Moses serves as an instructor and guest lecturer in the Educational Leadership Department. He also serves as a special advisor to the Dean of the College of Education.

Prior to accepting this appointment, Dr. Moses served on the board of the American College of Education. This College is located in Chicago, Illinois, and develops educational programs for teachers and administrators focusing on improving teacher training and executive leadership.

Dr. Moses has served in Texas public education for thirty years, starting as a teacher and administrator in the Duncanville and Garland school districts. He then served as superintendent for the Tatum, La Marque and Lubbock school districts.

From 1995 to 1999, Dr. Moses served as Texas Commissioner of Education, appointed twice by then Governor George W. Bush and confirmed by the Texas Senate. In 1999, he joined the Texas Tech University System as deputy chancellor. His responsibilities included governmental relations, development and institutional advancement, linkages between public education and the Texas Tech University system, and supervision of the news and publication division of the system as well as oversight of the Chancellor’s Council.

In October 2000, Mike Moses accepted the appointment as general superintendent of the Dallas Independent School District. He was selected unanimously for this position as the nineteenth superintendent of the district. He served in that capacity until assuming the role with Higher Ed Holdings in October 2004.

As the superintendent of the Dallas district, Dr. Moses spearheaded a successful school bond election in January 2002. As one of the largest bond elections in the nation, the $1.37 billion capital improvement program included renovations of existing schools and the construction of twenty new schools. Dr. Moses also led the Dallas district through the successful conclusion and release from federal court supervision of a thirty-three year old school desegregation court order. During his tenure, student achievement in the Dallas district markedly improved while the financial fund balance of the school district almost doubled.

Among awards that Dr. Moses has received are the Texas Business and Education’s “Distinguished Service Award” and the “Golden Deeds in Education Award” from Texas A & M University. The Texas School Public Relations Association awarded Dr. Moses its “1999 Key Communicator for Public Education Award”. Both the national and Texas PTA organizations have presented him with life memberships. He was also selected “Superintendent of the Year” in two Texas education regions and was one of the four national finalists for “Superintendent of the Year” in 2003.

His wife, Debi, is a former public school teacher and the couple has two sons.
